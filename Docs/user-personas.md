# Math Dash – User Personas

## 1. Child Learner – “Ava, Age 8”

- **Profile**
  - Age 8, Year 3 (KS2 start), uses a school Chromebook and a family tablet.
  - Enjoys quick games and light competition with friends or siblings.

- **Goals**
  - Feel confident with times tables, especially 7× and 8×.
  - Have “fun” practice instead of boring worksheets.
  - See that she’s “getting better” over time.

- **Frustrations**
  - Gets bored by slow or text‑heavy interfaces.
  - Feels discouraged when she doesn’t understand scores or progress.
  - Confused by cluttered layouts or too many options.

- **Key Needs from Math Dash**
  - Big, clear question and answers; quick rounds (30–120s).
  - Simple, positive feedback for right/wrong answers.
  - Small, visible “wins” (streaks, badges, new high scores).
  - Obvious topic choices (e.g., “2× table”, “Bonds to 10”).

---

## 2. Parent – “Ben, Age 39”

- **Profile**
  - Parent of two kids (ages 7 and 10); busy work schedule.
  - Tech‑comfortable but doesn’t want to learn complex dashboards.

- **Goals**
  - Provide short, evidence‑based daily practice for each child.
  - Quickly understand strengths/weaknesses (e.g., “7× table is weak”).
  - Make a low‑friction purchase decision if the app works.

- **Frustrations**
  - Confusing pricing and upgrades.
  - Over‑gamified apps that feel like pure entertainment.
  - Having to micro‑configure practice topics every time.

- **Key Needs from Math Dash**
  - Clear profile selection for each child.
  - Simple “My Progress” view per child with a topic proficiency summary.
  - Minimal but clear premium boundary: what’s free vs paid.
  - Safe, adult‑gated upgrade flow and no accidental child purchases.

---

## 3. Primary Teacher – “Ms. Khan, Age 32”

- **Profile**
  - Teaches Year 4; class of 28 pupils with mixed abilities.
  - Uses shared Chromebooks and an interactive whiteboard.

- **Goals**
  - Run a 5‑minute fluency warm‑up most days.
  - Track which pupils are struggling with specific tables or bonds.
  - Share simple progress summaries with parents or SEN coordinators.

- **Frustrations**
  - Set‑up friction (logins, codes, forgotten passwords).
  - Overly complex reporting dashboards.
  - Apps that are clearly “home‑only” and not classroom‑aware.

- **Key Needs from Math Dash**
  - Quickly create a class code (post‑MVP) and get pupils playing.
  - Class‑level summary with per‑pupil proficiency bands.
  - Offline‑tolerant play with sync when possible.
  - Clear differentiation between free/core and AI‑powered add‑ons.

---

## 4. Secondary Stakeholders

### 4.1 School Leaders

- Care about budgets, licensing simplicity, and evidence of impact.
- Need clean, exportable summaries (post‑MVP, via teacher tools).

### 4.2 Product & Data Team

- Need consistent events and clean domain models (see `requirements2.md`).
- Require architectures that scale cost‑effectively, especially for AI workloads.

---

## 5. Persona‑Driven Design Principles

- **Low friction first:** One‑tap “Play” from profile selection, with sensible defaults.
- **Kid‑appropriate feedback:** Simple language and clear color semantics for success/error.
- **Adult clarity:** Obvious free vs paid split and understandable progress summaries.
- **Classroom‑aware:** Profiles, multi‑child devices, and offline‑first by design.
